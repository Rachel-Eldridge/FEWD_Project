About Teachers Learning Connect

Character forged through learning

Some might call us a micro-school. We call ourselves 30 Acres: a one-room schoolhouse nestled in the farmlands of Elizabeth, Indiana.

Learning comes in all forms! Bible, good literature, Latin, mathematics, learning facts (Bible, history, etc.) in fun ways through music, art, story and drama, science with hands-on activities, penmanship -- yes, even cursive will be introduced and taught! Classical approach with a Charlotte Mason twist. Balance.

Meet the teachers

Mrs. Sheila has a BA in art education from the University of Cincinnati; several years of Christian classroom teaching (English, math, science, German, art, music, yearbook, physical education, and health!), home schooled children and taught art and music lessons for home schooled children. In the 2017-2018 school year, she did long-term substitution for 8th, 9th and 10th grade English, teaching Shakespeare. Currently, she teaches at KaySera Farm, including home school art lessons at the farm. She teaches flute at Lanesville, and private piano and flute lessons as well as preschool aged children and adult art lessons.

Mr. Doug is our resident polymath. He is a graduate of Taylor University in Political Science, Philosophy, and Business Administration. He has worked in the financial world for over 25 years. Currently, Mr. Doug writes weekly Bible studies and delivers them at the local library.

Good literature inspires great character. Speaking skills. Hands-on activities. Animal study. Shakespeare. Art and music as a conduit to deeper learning within other subjects but also within curriculum to be discussed as an academic which deepens the process called thinking.
